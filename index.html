Eggs are one of the most versatile, cost-effective protein sources and one of the most controversial foods.

Cholesterol Myths and Nutriton

In the words of Dr. David Perlmutter, "I don’t think there’s any more controversial food than eggs. By and large, the reason we have been told to avoid eating eggs is because they contain cholesterol, and indeed that’s true. A typical egg may contain as much as 200 mg of cholesterol. But does that mean we shouldn’t eat eggs?"

The research, published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, followed 177,000 individuals, for approximately 15 years. The results demonstrated that there was no significant associations between egg intake and blood lipids, mortality or major cardiovascular disease events.
